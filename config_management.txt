Configuration management is an organizational process that ensures all components of a system (hardware, software, documentation) work together seamlessly and consistently, conforming to specified requirements. Harvard Business Review +1. In other words, it's the "memory" that holds the details of everything in the system and how it connects to everything else.

Benefits of Configuration Management:
Improved control and visibility, reduced risk. It helps identify the impact of any change before it occurs, minimizing unexpected failures and enabling faster response times to incidents. It also ensures the system always operates according to specifications, guaranteeing quality and consistency.

Importance of Configuration Management: Efficient change management: In complex projects, it helps track changes to the system (such as software updates) and ensures they are properly documented and approved.

It aids in audits and regulatory compliance by providing accurate records for each component.

In conclusion: Configuration management is the backbone of any technological system's stability. It transforms chaos into organization, ensuring continuous and highly efficient operation.
